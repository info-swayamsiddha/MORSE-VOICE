# MORSE-VOICE
A free, browser-based assistive communication device for paralyzed individuals who can move their eyes but cannot speak.  
Turns any smartphone, tablet, or laptop into a voice using only the front camera and eye blinks.

---

## 🎯 Who Is This For?

- Patients with **locked-in syndrome**, **paralysis**, **ALS**, or **stroke** who retain eye movement and cognitive function
- Families who cannot afford expensive commercial eye-tracking devices ($5,000–$20,000)
- Caregivers who need a **portable, zero-installation** communication tool

---

## ✨ How It Works

1. **Open the web page** on any phone or tablet
2. **Point the front camera** at the patient's face (30–50 cm away)
3. **Calibrate once** — system learns the patient's open-eye and closed-eye state
4. **Blink to talk:**
   - **Short blink** (¼–⅔ sec) = **Dot (·)**
   - **Long blink** (¾–1½ sec) = **Dash (–)**
   - **Stop blinking 2 seconds** = Letter decoded
   - **Stop blinking 4 seconds** = Word spoken aloud
   - **6 short blinks** = End sentence, speak full message
5. The phone **beeps** for feedback and **speaks** the decoded text
